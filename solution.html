OUR SOLUTION

Solution Statement


Adulting Well will provide a helping hand to young adults caught in a cycle of anxiety or depression, by presenting them with a small, easy task that they can complete immediately.

Solution Characteristics


  • Shifting a person’s focus towards helping others can help reduce feelings of depression and anxiety [5]. Not E-lone will encourage depressed or anxious individuals to write short, anonymous messages of support to randomly-selected members. The note-passing feature will also provide instant motivation to young adults and teens by sending them messages of support throughout the day.


  • Not E-lone’s journaling activity will incentivize self-expression through encouraging the person to write down their current thoughts and feelings, which is a common therapeutic technique shown to ease feelings of anxiety and depression [6].


  • For young adults and teens struggling to complete their daily activities, Not E-lone’s First Step feature will help them get started.


  • Instead of overwhelming a depressed or anxious person with a large amount of work to do, Not E-lone will prompt them to input the first step towards completing their goal. The person will be unable to add more steps until the current activity is marked as finished.


  • The anxious or depressed person can later look back at the tasks they have completed throughout the day and feel a sense of achievement.


  • Not E-lone can use the list to generate a schedule for the next day, with possible challenges and goals depending on the activities completed and the individual’s temperament.


  • Some anxious individuals might prefer to see the final list of tasks. Not E-lone will streamline task management by providing those individuals with a quick schedule-builder.


  • Focusing on completing a goal can sometimes increase a person's stress levels and paradoxically reduce their ability to focus on that goal [7].


  • Organizing a list of tasks can help relieve that stress by shifting the individual’s focus towards creating a plan to complete the overall goal, instead of thinking about how the tasks are unfinished [7].


  • Reconceptualization involves presenting a person with a different way of thinking, and is another tool used by therapists to treat their clients [8]. Not E-lone will provide young adults with short stories designed to illustrate different perspectives to help them cope with their feelings of depression or anxiety.


  • Not E-lone will present each activity as part of a game, with a system of challenges and rewards for completing each activity. This system will help attract young adults and teens who would normally find these activities unappealing, as well as give them a reason to practice each activity on a regular basis.
